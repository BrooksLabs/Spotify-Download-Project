Phase 2: Downloading Your Library

5. Create a Dedicated Music Folder
You don't want 10,000 files dumping onto your desktop.

    Create a new folder named "Spotify Library" exactly where you want the music saved.

    In your terminal, type cd  (make sure to include the space after 'cd').

    Drag and drop that new "Spotify Library" folder directly into the terminal window. The terminal will automatically type out the folder path for you. Hit Enter. Your terminal is now "inside" that folder.

6. The Download Command
Go to Spotify, find the playlist you want to start with and click the three dots, select "Share", and click "Copy link to playlist".

In your terminal, type spotdl  followed by the link you just copied, but add --user-auth to the very end. It should look like this:
spotdl https://open.spotify.com/playlist/YOUR_PLAYLIST_LINK_HEREs --user-auth

7. Authorize in Your Browser

    The terminal will pause, and a Spotify login page will automatically pop up in your web browser.

    Log in with your normal Spotify account and click Agree to grant spotDL permission to view your playlists.

    The browser will say "Success" or redirect you to a blank page. You can close that browser tab.

8. Let it Run
Press Enter. spotDL will now fetch the metadata, find the best audio matches, and save perfectly tagged MP3 files right into your folder.


    Pro-Tip for large batches: If your internet cuts out, or if you want to stop the process so you can close your laptop, simply click into the terminal and press Ctrl + C. When you are ready to resume, run the exact same command from Step 6. spotDL generates a tracking file, so it will instantly skip everything it already downloaded and pick up right where it left off.
